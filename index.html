Our Story

A Home Away From Home

The Colburn, Catterick and Richmond Fijian Community was formed over 15 years ago by Fijian families and soldiers stationed at Catterick Garrison. What began as informal Sunday gatherings has grown into a vibrant, structured community — proud of its Pacific Island roots while fully embraced in North Yorkshire life.

We come together to celebrate language, traditions, faith, and love of sport, while supporting one another through the unique challenges of military family life far from home.

Get Involved

Our Mission

To preserve Fijian culture, support families and youth, and foster a strong, united community in North Yorkshire.

Supporting Soldiers

Many members serve in the British Army. We provide pastoral care, social events, and a home away from home for serving personnel and families.

Empowering Youth

Our young people are our future. We run mentoring, education support, and youth clubs to help Fijian children thrive in British communities.

Family First

From welcoming new arrivals to organising family days and feasts, we ensure no family is left isolated or alone.

Heritage

Our Culture

Fijian culture is rich, warm, and deeply communal. Here's a glimpse into the traditions we carry with us to Yorkshire.

🏺

iTaukei Language

The iTaukei language is the soul of our identity. We run language sessions for children and adults to keep our mother tongue alive across generations born in Britain.

🍖

Food & the Lovo

Food is the heart of Fijian culture. The lovo — an earth oven — prepares whole meats and root vegetables for communal feasts. Kokoda, rourou, and cassava remind us of home.

🌿

Kava Ceremony

The sevusevu kava ceremony is a sacred tradition used to welcome guests and mark important occasions. We conduct traditional kava ceremonies at all major community gatherings.

💃

Meke Dance & Music

Meke is traditional Fijian performance — dance, song, and storytelling combined. Our community performs meke at Fiji Day, cultural shows, and school events, keeping this ancient art form alive.

⚓

Fijian Values

Kerekere (communal sharing), veidomoni (respect), and sautu (peace and harmony) are core values that guide how we treat each other and serve our wider community.

📖

Stories & Oral History

Our elders share the history of Fiji through storytelling — tales of the islands, ancestry, the sea, and spiritual traditions. We record these for future generations born in Britain.

Our Future

Youth & Families

Investing in the next generation of Fijian-British young people and supporting every family in our network.

Education Support

Free tutoring and study support sessions for Fijian students in primary and secondary school. We work with local schools to ensure no child is left behind.

  • After-school homework clubs
  • GCSE and A-Level revision sessions
  • Scholarship & university guidance

Youth Mentoring

Experienced community mentors work one-on-one with young people aged 12–21 on career guidance, personal development, and cultural identity.

  • Monthly mentor meetings
  • CV writing and interview skills
  • Cultural identity workshops

Kids' Activities

A fun-packed programme for younger children combining sport, arts and crafts, Fijian language play, and storytelling every fortnight.

  • Mini rugby & netball sessions
  • Fijian arts & crafts club
  • Language play sessions (ages 4–10)

Family Welcome & Support

New Fijian families arriving in the area are welcomed with a dedicated support package covering schools, housing, services, and social integration.

  • New arrival welcome packs
  • Signposting to local services
  • Befriending & buddy schemes

Women's Circle

A safe, supportive space for Fijian women to connect, share experiences, and access wellbeing resources — meeting monthly over shared food and fellowship.

  • Monthly coffee mornings
  • Health & wellbeing workshops
  • Crafts, cooking, and culture

Summer & Holiday Camps

Annual outdoor adventure and cultural camps for young people. Camping, bushcraft, sports, Fijian storytelling and campfire cooking. Unforgettable memories.

  • Annual summer camp (ages 8–18)
  • Easter activity days
  • Christmas celebration for kids
